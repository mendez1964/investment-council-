# Trading Journal Template

## Why a Journal Is Non-Negotiable

The trading journal is the single most powerful improvement tool available to any trader. It is more valuable than any indicator, any screener, any course. Here is why:

Without a journal, you remember wins clearly and losses vaguely. You believe you are executing your plan when you are not. You repeat the same mistakes because you do not see the pattern.

With a journal, patterns become undeniable. You see that you always enter too early on breakouts. You see that you are profitable on Tuesday mornings and terrible on Friday afternoons. You see that your best trades are held for 3-5 days and your worst trades are held for 3-5 weeks. You see you are consistently profitable on momentum setups and consistently losing on reversal plays. You cannot ignore a pattern when it is right in front of you in writing.

## Post-Trade Review (Fill Out Same Day — While Fresh)

**Did you follow your plan?** (Yes / Partially / No)

**If you deviated from the plan, why?** (Be honest)

**What did you do well?**

**What would you do differently?**

**Quality of the Setup:** (Was this a legitimate setup or did you force a trade?)

**Quality of the Execution:** (Did you enter and exit well? Or did you chase/panic?)

**Emotional State During the Trade:** (Calm / Anxious / Overconfident / Fearful / Neutral)

**If it was a losing trade — was the loss taken at the correct stop?**
- If yes: The system worked. This was an expected loss. Move on.
- If no: Why did you hold past your stop? This is the most important question.

**If it was a winning trade — did you take profits at your planned target?**
- If no — did you hold longer because the trade was working well, or because you got greedy?

**What is the main lesson from this trade?**

## Key Patterns to Watch For Over Time

- **Time of day edge:** Are you better in the morning, midday, or afternoon?
- **Day of week edge:** Any day consistently better or worse?
- **Setup type edge:** Which setup type is most profitable for you?
- **Hold time edge:** Are your best trades held for a specific duration?
- **Position size edge:** Do you perform better with smaller or larger positions?
- **Market condition edge:** Better in trending markets or volatile markets?

These patterns are your personal edge — use them to concentrate your trading in conditions where you historically perform best.
