## The Methodology: Forensic Primary Source Analysis

### The Core Discipline
Burry reads every word of every document relevant to an investment. Not summaries. Not analyst notes. Not management presentations. The actual primary source documents.

**In 2005, he requested the actual prospectuses for mortgage-backed securities.** These were documents running 130+ pages each, containing the actual loan-level characteristics of every mortgage in the pool. He read hundreds of them.

What he found that Wall Street was ignoring:
- Teaser rates of 1-2% that would reset to 7-8% in 2-3 years
- "Stated income" loans — no income verification, the borrower just stated a number
- Combined loan-to-value ratios above 100% (the borrower owed more than the home was worth on day one)
- Prepayment penalties that trapped borrowers in loans they couldn't escape
- Geographic concentration in the most bubbly markets — California, Florida, Nevada
- Originators who had zero economic stake in whether the loan performed

**The rating agencies had not read these documents.** They were using models based on historical default rates from a period when home prices had never declined nationally. Burry applied common sense: what happens when rates reset and these borrowers cannot afford the new payment and cannot refinance because their home is worth less than their loan?

The answer was obvious — to anyone who actually read the documents.

### The Primary Source Principle Applied to Any Investment
Burry's methodology can be applied to any investment, not just mortgage securities:

1. **Identify what consensus assumes.** What does the market price imply must be true?
2. **Go to the primary source to test that assumption.** Don't read the summary — read the source.
3. **Ask: what if the core assumption is wrong?** Model the downside scenario fully.
4. **Find the asymmetric trade.** If you are right, the upside is large. If you are wrong, the downside is defined and limited.

## How to Apply Burry Analysis Today

### Step 1: Identify the Consensus Assumption
For any investment thesis, ask: What must be true for the current valuation to make sense? What is everyone assuming?

Examples:
- For a high-growth tech stock: Revenue must grow 30%+ annually for 5+ years AND margins must expand significantly
- For a bank stock: Credit quality remains benign AND net interest margins stay wide
- For an index at current valuations: Earnings grow at trend AND interest rates stay accommodative

### Step 2: Stress Test the Assumption
What happens if the core assumption is wrong by 20%? By 50%? Completely?

Burry's test for the mortgage trade: What happens if home prices fall 20% nationally? (Something that had "never happened" — which means the models had never been stress-tested for it.) The answer: the entire subprime market collapses, taking the financial system with it.

### Step 3: Find What Nobody Is Reading
**For equities:**
- Read the 10-K footnotes — especially litigation, pension obligations, lease obligations, off-balance-sheet items
- Read the proxy statement — executive compensation structure, related party transactions
- Read competitor filings — what do competitors say about the industry that management is not disclosing?
- Read industry-specific regulatory filings

**For bonds and credit:**
- Read the actual indenture, not the summary
- Understand every covenant and what happens when violated
- Model the recovery rate if the company defaults

**For any position:**
- What is the bear case? Not the soft bear case but the catastrophic bear case?
- What single assumption does the entire bull thesis depend on?

### Index Fund Bubble Warning (2019)
Burry made news by warning that index funds had created a passive investing bubble similar to the CDO bubble of 2007. His argument: the same stocks keep getting more money regardless of valuation (because they have the largest market caps and thus the largest index weightings), creating distortions similar to how CDO demand inflated mortgage prices regardless of loan quality.

This is a classic Burry analysis: identify the mechanism of distortion, model the unwinding scenario, determine whether the distortion is self-correcting or self-reinforcing until it suddenly reverses.

## The Burry Checklist

1. **What is everyone assuming?** What is the consensus view that is pricing this asset?
2. **Have I gone to the actual primary source?** Have I read the actual filing, the actual prospectus, the actual contract — not a summary?
3. **What do the footnotes say?** Financial statements are marketing. Footnotes are disclosure.
4. **What single assumption does the bull thesis depend on?** What happens if it is wrong by 50%? By 100%?
5. **What is the catastrophic downside scenario nobody is modeling?**
6. **Does the trade structure limit my downside?** Am I structured to be wrong without being destroyed?
7. **What information exists that people are not reading?** Where is the undiscovered evidence?
8. **Am I being contrarian for a reason, or just being contrarian?** The reason must be rigorous evidence, not opinion.
