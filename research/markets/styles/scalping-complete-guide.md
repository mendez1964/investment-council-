# Scalping — Complete Guide

## What Scalping Is
Scalping holds positions for seconds to a few minutes, targeting very small price moves (0.1-0.5%) repeated many times per day. Requires the fastest execution, tightest risk management, and most mental discipline of any trading style. Not suitable for most people.

---

## Scalping Requirements

**Technology:**
- Fastest possible internet connection
- Direct access order routing (not market maker routing)
- Level 2 quotes in real-time
- Time and Sales (the tape) running continuously
- 1-minute and 5-minute charts simultaneously

**Platform:** Thinkorswim or Interactive Brokers for direct access. Webull for free Level 2.

**Commissions:** Scalpers need the lowest possible commissions. Even $0.005 per share matters at scale.

## The Key Scalping Setups

### VWAP Scalp
The most reliable scalping setup. Price at VWAP provides the tightest risk:

**Long scalp at VWAP:**
- Price pulls back to VWAP during an uptrending morning
- Volume dries up at VWAP (no more sellers)
- A 1-minute bullish candle forms — hammer, bullish engulfing, or strong green close
- Entry: Break above that candle's high
- Stop: Below VWAP by 0.1-0.2%
- Target: 0.3-0.5% above entry

**Short scalp at VWAP:**
- Price in a downtrend rallies to VWAP
- Volume picks back up as price touches VWAP from below (sellers defending)
- A red 1-minute candle or shooting star forms at VWAP
- Entry: Break below that candle's low
- Stop: Above VWAP by 0.1-0.2%
- Target: 0.3-0.5% below entry

### Opening Range Micro-Scalp
In the first 1-3 minutes, the first 1-minute candle defines the micro range. Breaking above the high of that first candle with a large volume green candle is a scalp long. Breaking below the low is a scalp short.

### Level 2 Order Book Imbalance
When the Level 2 shows a large bid with no comparable offer (buyers stacked, sellers absent), price is likely to move up. Enter immediately, target the next visible offer layer. Exit when the imbalance disappears.

---

## Risk Per Scalp
Never risk more than 0.1-0.2% of account on any single scalp trade. Scalpers take many trades — even a small risk per trade compounds if you are wrong repeatedly.

**Daily loss limit for scalpers:** 0.5-1% of account. If you hit it, stop for the day.

---

## When NOT to Scalp

- Low volume: Wide spreads eat profits
- Major news pending: Price gaps through stops unpredictably
- Choppy price action around VWAP: Random noise in both directions
- After 11:30am and before 2:00pm: Dead zone, volume too low
- When you are already down for the day: Emotional state compromises decisions

---

## The Scalper's Mindset
Scalpers must accept losses instantly and emotionlessly. A scalp that hits its stop gets exited in under a second — no hesitation. A scalp that reaches its target gets exited immediately — no hoping for more. The discipline of executing the plan without emotion is everything.
