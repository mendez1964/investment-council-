# Swing Trading — Complete Guide

## What Swing Trading Is
Swing trading holds positions for days to weeks (typically 3-15 trading days). The goal: capture one "swing" in a trending stock — the move from support to resistance in an uptrend, or resistance to support in a downtrend. This style suits people who cannot watch screens all day but want more active involvement than long-term investing.

---

## The Core Approach

Swing traders use daily charts as their primary analysis tool and look for high-probability setups where:
1. The larger trend is established (weekly chart confirms direction)
2. A short-term pullback or consolidation has created an entry opportunity
3. A clear risk level exists (stop loss location is obvious)
4. The potential move to the next resistance level is at least 2-3x the risk

## The Primary Setups

### Setup 1: Pullback to Rising 20 EMA
The most reliable swing trading entry in an uptrend.

**Conditions required:**
- Stock in a clear uptrend (20 EMA rising, price above 50 SMA, 50 SMA above 200 SMA)
- Stock pulls back from recent high
- Pullback touches or approaches the rising 20 EMA
- Volume dries up during the pullback (sellers exhausted)
- A reversal candle forms at the 20 EMA (hammer, bullish engulfing, doji)

**Entry:** Buy when the reversal candle's high is exceeded with volume returning
**Stop loss:** Below the low of the reversal candle (or below the 20 EMA by 1-2%)
**Target:** Previous high, then 38.2% Fibonacci extension, then 61.8% extension

**Why it works:** The market is in an uptrend (higher probability of continuation). The pullback to 20 EMA represents the best entry price within the trend. Volume dry-up confirms sellers are exhausted. The reversal candle confirms buyers are re-entering.

### Setup 2: Breakout from Multi-Week Base
When a stock has been consolidating in a tight range for 3+ weeks and breaks out on volume.

**Conditions required:**
- Stock has been ranging for at least 15-20 trading days
- Volume during the base has been declining (contraction)
- Price is near the upper boundary of the range
- Breakout day has significantly elevated volume (2x average minimum)
- The base is not more than 30-35% deep from prior high

**Entry:** Buy the breakout above the base resistance on volume, or buy the retest of the breakout level (within 1-2 days after the break)
**Stop loss:** Below the base support (the low of the range)
**Target:** Measure the height of the base and project it upward from the breakout

**Volume is critical:** Low-volume breakouts from bases fail 60-70% of the time. High-volume breakouts succeed much more often. Do not enter without volume confirmation.

### Setup 3: Bull Flag After Strong Move
After a strong, fast move up (the flagpole), stock consolidates in a tight, slightly downward-sloping channel.

**Conditions required:**
- Initial move was strong (8-15% or more in a short period)
- Consolidation is tight — price is not giving back much of the gain
- Volume during the flag is declining
- Consolidation duration: 3-15 trading days

**Entry:** Break above the upper boundary of the flag channel on volume
**Stop loss:** Below the lower boundary of the flag channel
**Target:** Add the flagpole length to the breakout point

### Setup 4: Oversold Bounce with Volume
After a sharp decline in a fundamentally sound stock, buy the reversal.

**Conditions required:**
- Stock in a longer-term uptrend (above 200 SMA)
- Sharp decline of 15-25% in short period, making it technically oversold
- RSI below 30 on daily chart
- Volume surge on a reversal candle (selling climax or buying surge)
- Stock not in secular downtrend — this is a healthy stock having a bad week/month

**Entry:** Buy the reversal candle on volume
**Stop loss:** Below the reversal candle's low
**Target:** Previous support levels, 20 EMA, then 50 SMA

## Weekly Chart First — Daily Chart Entry

The professional swing trading process starts with the weekly chart.

**Weekly chart assessment:**
- Is the primary trend up, down, or sideways?
- Where are the major support and resistance levels?
- What is the weekly RSI reading? (Above 50 = bullish, below 50 = bearish)
- Is this a stock making higher highs and higher lows (healthy) or lower highs and lower lows (sick)?

**If the weekly chart is bullish:** Look for entry opportunities on the daily chart — all four setups above become valid candidates.

**If the weekly chart is bearish or neutral:** Do not take long swings regardless of how attractive the daily chart looks. The weekly chart is the tide. The daily chart is the wave. Never swim against the tide.

## Position Sizing for Swing Trades

**Standard swing trade sizing:**
- Risk per trade: 1-2% of account
- Typical stop distance: 3-8% below entry
- Typical position size: 10-30% of account value (depending on stop width)

**Example:** $100,000 account, 1.5% risk = $1,500 risk per trade.
Stop is 5% below entry at $50 = stop at $47.50 ($2.50 risk per share)
Position size: $1,500 / $2.50 = 600 shares = $30,000 position (30% of account)

**The principle:** Wide stops force small positions. Tight stops allow large positions. Always calculate correctly.

## Managing Swing Trades — The Three Rules

**Rule 1: Let winners run to the target.** Do not sell early because you are nervous about losing paper profits. You sized the trade for a specific target — give it a chance to reach it. Scale out: sell 1/3 at first target, let remainder run with a trailing stop.

**Rule 2: Honor your stop loss.** When price hits your predetermined stop, exit. Do not lower the stop to "give it more room." The stop exists for a reason — the trade is wrong at that price.

**Rule 3: Know the upcoming earnings date.** Earnings can cause enormous gaps. Either:
- Exit the position before earnings if you do not want binary risk
- Reduce position size to half if you want to maintain some exposure through earnings
- Deliberately play earnings as a separate strategy with defined risk (options)

---

## Earnings Avoidance vs. Earnings Plays

**Earnings avoidance:** The conservative approach. Close swing positions 2-3 days before earnings to eliminate gap risk. Re-enter after earnings if the stock reacts well and the setup is still intact.

**Earnings play:** Deliberately enter before earnings because:
- The stock has been building toward earnings (pre-earnings run)
- Options provide defined-risk exposure through the event
- You have high conviction the results will surprise positively

The Investment Council does not recommend blind earnings plays without clear framework support.

## Crypto Swing Trading Specifics

Crypto swings work with the same setups but with important differences:
- Much higher volatility — position size accordingly (smaller)
- No earnings announcements — but major protocol events, regulatory news, and macro events play a similar role
- Weekend trading — crypto markets never close. Gaps on Monday morning don't exist for crypto.
- Bitcoin dominance as a sector rotation indicator: When Bitcoin is strong relative to altcoins, follow Bitcoin. When altcoins are outperforming, cycle rotation is occurring.
- Fibonacci retracement levels tend to work well in crypto — apply to major cycle swings
