# Position Trading — Complete Guide

## What Position Trading Is
Position trading holds for weeks to months (typically 1-6 months), capturing larger trend moves. Combines fundamental analysis to find what to own with technical analysis to determine when to enter and exit. The sweet spot where the Buffett/Lynch fundamental frameworks meet the Tudor Jones/Livermore technical frameworks.

---

## The Core Approach

Position traders are looking for stocks that have:
1. Strong fundamental stories (Buffett/Lynch quality check)
2. Powerful macro tailwinds (Dalio cycle alignment)
3. Technical confirmation of the trend (Livermore/Tudor Jones confirmation)
4. Beginning or middle of a major trend (not the end)

---

## Identifying Position Trade Candidates

**Fundamental filter first:**
- Earnings growing at 15%+ annually
- Revenue growth confirming earnings growth
- Expanding margins (improving business economics)
- Strong return on equity
- Balance sheet can support growth
- Lynch category: Fast grower or turnaround in progress

**Technical confirmation second:**
- Weekly chart in clear uptrend
- 50-week MA rising
- Price above both 50 and 200 SMA on daily chart
- Stock making new highs or breaking out from a long base
- Volume expanding on advances, contracting on pullbacks

**Macro alignment third (Dalio check):**
- Does the sector benefit from the current macro environment?
- Is the sector in favor with institutional money (fund flows)?

## Entry Methods for Position Trades

### The Stage 2 Base Breakout (Stan Weinstein)
One of the most powerful position trading setups:

**Stage 1:** Basing — stock has been moving sideways, 50-week MA is flat
**Stage 2:** Advancing — stock breaks above the base on strong volume, 50-week MA begins to rise. THIS IS THE ENTRY.
**Stage 3:** Topping — stock's advance slows, starts trading sideways at high, volume erratic
**Stage 4:** Declining — stock breaks below 50-week MA on volume. SELL.

The Stage 2 breakout captures the entire advancing phase of a stock's cycle. This method, outlined in Stan Weinstein's "Secrets for Profiting in Bull and Bear Markets," is one of the cleanest position trading frameworks ever developed.

**Entry:** Buy the break above the Stage 1 base on volume
**Stop:** Below the base support level
**Target:** Hold through all of Stage 2 until Stage 3 topping signs appear

### Buying the Strong Pullback in Stage 2
Once a Stage 2 advance is underway, pullbacks to the rising 50 SMA provide second-chance entries:
**Entry:** Stock in Stage 2 pulls back to rising 50 SMA, volume dries up, reversal candle forms
**Stop:** Below the 50 SMA
**Target:** New high beyond the prior peak, then extended Fibonacci targets

## Building Positions Over Time

Unlike day or swing trading where you size up immediately, position traders often build over time:

**Initial position:** 25-33% of planned total allocation on first entry
**Add on strength:** If the thesis is confirmed (stock advances, fundamentals confirm), add another 33%
**Full position:** When the trade has proved itself, add the remainder

**Benefit:** Average entry price is better than buying all at once, and your largest size is on when the trade is working — not when you are uncertain.

## Managing Through Volatility

Position traders must endure normal volatility without panic-selling. The 20% intraday move that terrifies a day trader is just noise to a well-analyzed position trade.

**The volatility tolerance test:** Can you watch your position drop 15% on a bad week and not panic? If not, your position size is too large or your conviction in the fundamental story is too low.

**Holding through normal volatility:** A stock in a strong uptrend can correct 15-20% and still be fully intact from a trend perspective. The 50 SMA is the key — as long as the stock holds the 50 SMA and the 50 SMA is still rising, the position trade is intact.

**Exiting a position trade:**
- 50 SMA breaks (price closes significantly below rising 50 SMA)
- 50 SMA itself begins to flatten or turn downward
- Volume increases on down days and decreases on up days (distribution)
- Fundamental story changes (earnings decelerate significantly, competitive threat emerges)
- Original thesis no longer valid

## Options Integration for Position Trades

**Protecting large gains:** When a position trade has moved significantly in your favor and you want to protect gains without selling, buy put options. A put with a strike below your cost basis essentially creates a "floor" — you keep all upside while limiting downside.

**Selling covered calls (for income):** When a position is consolidating and you expect sideways action, sell covered calls above the current price. If the stock does not reach the strike, you collect premium. If it does, you sell the stock at the strike price (which is above current price anyway).
