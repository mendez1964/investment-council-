# Volume Analysis — Complete Reference

## Why Volume Is the Most Important Confirmation Tool

Price tells you what happened. Volume tells you why it happened and whether to believe it.

A stock price moving up with high volume means many participants are actively buying — the move is backed by conviction. The same price move on low volume means few participants are involved — the move may not sustain.

Jesse Livermore said: "Watch the volume, it tells the truth."

---

## The Core Principle: Volume Confirms Price

**High volume + price advance = strong, believable move**
**Low volume + price advance = weak, suspicious move**
**High volume + price decline = strong selling pressure, respect it**
**Low volume + price decline = weak selling, likely to find support**

## Volume Characteristics of Key Market Events

### Breakouts
**High conviction breakout:** Stock has been consolidating for weeks. Volume has been declining throughout the consolidation (no one interested). Then on the breakout day, volume surges to 2x, 3x, or more above the average. This surge tells you that new buyers are flooding in with conviction.

**Low conviction breakout (trap):** Same setup but price breaks out on volume that is only slightly above average or even below average. This is the classic false breakout. Without the volume surge, there is no real conviction behind the move. The stock will likely fall back into the range.

**Rule:** Do not trade breakouts without volume confirmation. Period.

### Climax Volume (Exhaustion)
After an extended trend, a massive volume spike often marks the END of the move rather than the continuation.

**Selling climax:** At the bottom of a prolonged decline, a single massive-volume down day where sellers exhaust themselves. This is the "final flush" — every weak holder is forced out simultaneously. This climax selling often marks the exact bottom or close to it. High conviction buy signal when occurring after a significant decline.

**Buying climax:** At the top of a prolonged advance, a massive-volume up day or week where buyers rush in with euphoria. Smart money distributes into this frenzy. This climax buying often marks the exact top or close to it. High conviction sell/short signal when occurring after a significant advance.

**Identifying climax volume:** The day's or week's volume is notably the highest in many months. The candle often has a long wick as price initially moves sharply in the trend direction and then reverses.

### Volume Dry-Up Before Breakouts
One of the most bullish setups:

Stock has been in a prolonged downtrend. Price stabilizes and begins forming a base. Volume during this basing period gradually declines to near multi-month lows. This volume dry-up means sellers are exhausted — there is no one left who wants to sell at this level. The stock is being quietly accumulated.

When volume finally returns with a price advance, the move often has significant follow-through because there is virtually no supply overhead.

### High Volume Down Days in an Uptrend
When a stock in an uptrend has a large volume red candle, this is a warning signal. It means sellers are asserting themselves more forcefully than the trend might suggest.

**Distribution days:** A significant down day on volume that is higher than the previous day. When a market index has 4-5 distribution days within a few weeks, it is often signaling a market top.

## On Balance Volume (OBV)

### What It Is
Developed by Joe Granville. Adds volume on up days and subtracts volume on down days. Creates a cumulative line that reflects whether volume is flowing into or out of a stock.

### How to Use OBV
OBV should track the general direction of price. When it does, the trend is confirmed.

**Bullish divergence in OBV:** Price makes a new low but OBV does not — it stays flat or rises. More buyers are absorbing shares at the new low than were present at the previous low. This is accumulation. Bullish.

**Bearish divergence in OBV:** Price makes a new high but OBV does not — it fails to reach its previous high. Volume is not confirming the price advance. Distribution occurring. Bearish.

**Breakout confirmation:** When price breaks out of a range and OBV also makes a new high, the breakout is confirmed by volume flow — high conviction.

## Accumulation/Distribution Line

Similar to OBV but more sophisticated — it considers where price closes within the day's range, not just whether the day was up or down.

**Logic:** If price closes near the high of the day, buyers were in control even if the overall day was a down day. The A/D line credits partial buying pressure.

**A/D Line rising while price consolidates:** Smart money accumulating quietly. Bullish signal.
**A/D Line falling while price rises:** Smart money distributing into price strength. Bearish warning.

## Relative Volume (RVOL)

Compares today's volume to the average volume for that time of day or that day of the week.

**RVOL of 2.0:** Today's volume is double the typical volume at this time — something significant is happening.
**RVOL of 0.5:** Today's volume is half the typical — quiet day, no conviction.

**Practical use for day traders:**
- Before entering a momentum trade, check RVOL
- RVOL above 1.5 or 2.0 confirms there is participation behind the move
- Low RVOL moves are suspect and more likely to reverse

## Volume Spread Analysis (VSA)

Developed by Tom Williams, based on Richard Wyckoff's work. VSA analyzes the relationship between the spread (range) of a candle, its close position within that range, and the volume to determine whether smart money is buying or selling.

### Key VSA Concepts

**No Supply (Ultra-Low Volume Down Bar):** A small range red candle on very low volume. This tells you there is almost no supply at this price level. Sellers have dried up. Price is likely to rise.

**No Demand (Ultra-Low Volume Up Bar):** A small range green candle on very low volume. Almost no buyers are interested. Price is likely to fall.

**Stopping Volume:** High volume on a down bar, especially near a support level. The high volume suggests that a major buyer is absorbing all the selling — "stopping" the decline. This buyer has a large appetite at this price level. Often precedes a significant rally.

**Upthrust:** Price briefly spikes above a resistance level on high volume but closes back below it. Smart money tests whether there is real buying above resistance. When there is not (the upthrust reversal), it signals a bearish move is coming.

## Volume Profile

### What It Is
Displays the distribution of volume at each price level over a period of time (as opposed to over time horizontally). Creates a histogram on the side of the chart.

### Key Volume Profile Levels

**Point of Control (POC):** The price level with the highest volume traded. This is the most accepted price — where the most transactions occurred. POC acts as a strong magnet — price tends to return to it.

**Value Area High (VAH):** The upper boundary of where 70% of volume was traded. Acts as resistance.

**Value Area Low (VAL):** The lower boundary of where 70% of volume was traded. Acts as support.

**High Volume Nodes (HVN):** Price ranges where a lot of trading occurred. Act as support and resistance because there are many traders who bought or sold there and remember the price.

**Low Volume Nodes (LVN):** Price ranges where very little trading occurred. Price tends to move quickly through these areas — no memory, no support or resistance.

### Trading Volume Profile
If price is above the POC and approaches it from above: POC acts as support. Buy near POC with stop below.
If price is below the POC and approaches it from below: POC acts as resistance. Sell near POC with stop above.
Price moving from LVN toward HVN: Will likely move quickly and then slow at the HVN.
