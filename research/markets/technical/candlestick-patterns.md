# Candlestick Patterns — Complete Reference

## How to Read a Candlestick
Each candle represents a time period. The body shows where price opened and closed. The wicks (shadows) show the high and low. A green/white candle: close above open (buyers won). A red/black candle: close below open (sellers won).

**Key principle:** Context determines meaning. The same candle pattern means completely different things at different locations on a chart.

## Single Candle Patterns

### Doji Variants
A doji forms when open and close are at the same price — a cross shape. It signals indecision — neither buyers nor sellers won the period.

**Standard Doji:** Body is tiny or nonexistent. Equal wicks above and below. Means: indecision. Significance depends entirely on location.
- At resistance after an uptrend: potentially bearish — buyers exhausted
- At support after a downtrend: potentially bullish — sellers exhausted
- In the middle of a range: meaningless

**Long-Legged Doji:** Very long wicks above and below with tiny body. Extreme indecision. Price traveled far in both directions and ended where it started. High volatility session with no net progress. Significant at major levels.

**Gravestone Doji:** Long wick above, no lower wick. Opens and closes at the low of the day. Price rallied but was completely rejected — sellers drove price all the way back to the open. Bearish signal, especially at resistance after an uptrend.

**Dragonfly Doji:** Long wick below, no upper wick. Opens and closes at the high of the day. Price dropped but was completely recovered — buyers absorbed every seller. Bullish signal, especially at support after a downtrend.

### Hammer and Hanging Man
**Same pattern, opposite implications based on location:**

Both have: Small body near the top of the candle, long lower wick (at least 2x the body), little or no upper wick.

**Hammer:** Appears at the bottom of a downtrend. Color doesn't matter much (green is slightly stronger). The long lower wick shows sellers pushed price down significantly but buyers drove it back up — bullish reversal signal. Volume should be above average to confirm.

**Hanging Man:** Identical shape but appears at the top of an uptrend. The long lower wick shows buying occurred but price could not hold — distribution. Bearish reversal signal. Requires confirmation from the next candle (a red candle).

### Shooting Star and Inverted Hammer
**Same pattern, opposite implications based on location:**

Both have: Small body near the bottom of the candle, long upper wick (at least 2x the body), little or no lower wick.

**Shooting Star:** At the top of an uptrend. Price rallied significantly but was fully rejected — sellers overwhelmed buyers. Bearish. Looks like a shooting star falling. Stronger when red/black.

**Inverted Hammer:** At the bottom of a downtrend. Price attempted a rally — shows buyers are beginning to fight back. Needs confirmation. Bullish potential.

### Marubozu (No Wicks)
**Bullish Marubozu:** Large green candle, opens at the low and closes at the high. No wicks. Buyers dominated the entire session without pause. Strong bullish signal, especially on a breakout.

**Bearish Marubozu:** Large red candle, opens at the high and closes at the low. No wicks. Sellers dominated the entire session without pause. Strong bearish signal, especially on a breakdown.

### Spinning Tops
Small body (green or red), with wicks above and below roughly equal to or longer than the body. Different from doji because there IS a meaningful body, but neither side dominated decisively. Indecision. Same context rules as doji apply.

## Multi-Candle Patterns

### Engulfing Patterns

**Bullish Engulfing:** Two candles. First is a small red candle. Second is a large green candle whose body completely engulfs the first candle's body. Buyers overwhelmed sellers in the second period, absorbing all of the previous session's selling and more. Strong bullish reversal signal when it appears after a downtrend. Volume on the second candle should be high.

**Bearish Engulfing:** Two candles. First is a small green candle. Second is a large red candle whose body completely engulfs the first candle's body. Sellers overwhelmed buyers. Strong bearish reversal signal when it appears after an uptrend.

**Key amplifiers:** The larger the body of the engulfing candle relative to the first candle, the stronger the signal. Occurring at a known support or resistance level doubles the significance.

### Morning Star and Evening Star

**Morning Star (Bullish):** Three candles.
1. First: Large red candle (sellers in control)
2. Second: Small body candle (either color, or doji) gapping down — indecision or reversal point
3. Third: Large green candle that closes well into the first candle's body

The three-candle sequence tells a story: sellers dominated, then uncertainty, then buyers took over decisively. Strong reversal signal at bottoms.

**Evening Star (Bearish):** Three candles.
1. First: Large green candle (buyers in control)
2. Second: Small body candle gapping up — sellers begin to question the move
3. Third: Large red candle that closes well into the first candle's body

Strong reversal signal at tops.

### Three White Soldiers and Three Black Crows

**Three White Soldiers:** Three consecutive large green candles, each opening within the previous candle's body and closing near its high. Strong and sustained buying over three periods. Bullish continuation or reversal signal. Most powerful when appearing after a base or at the end of a prolonged downtrend.

**Three Black Crows:** Three consecutive large red candles, each opening within the previous candle's body and closing near its low. Strong sustained selling. Bearish continuation or reversal signal. Most powerful at the end of a prolonged uptrend.

### Harami Patterns (Inside Bar)

**Bullish Harami:** Two candles. First is a large red candle. Second is a small green candle whose body is completely inside the first candle's body. The selling momentum slowed dramatically. Indecision after a strong move down. Not as strong as engulfing — needs confirmation.

**Bearish Harami:** First is a large green candle. Second is a small red candle inside the first. Buying momentum slowed. Needs confirmation candle.

### Tweezer Tops and Bottoms
Two or more candles with the same high (tweezer top) or same low (tweezer bottom).

**Tweezer Top:** Two candles reaching the exact same high — sellers defended that level twice. Support becomes a ceiling. Bearish at resistance.

**Tweezer Bottom:** Two candles reaching the exact same low — buyers defended that level twice. Bullish at support.

### Dark Cloud Cover and Piercing Line

**Dark Cloud Cover (Bearish):** Two candles. First is a large green candle. Second opens above the first candle's high but closes below the midpoint of the first candle's body. Sellers drove price down aggressively through half the previous gain. Bearish at resistance.

**Piercing Line (Bullish):** Two candles. First is a large red candle. Second opens below the first candle's low but closes above the midpoint of the first candle's body. Buyers recovered more than half the previous loss. Bullish at support.

## Context Rules — Never Trade Patterns in Isolation

### Rule 1: Location Is Everything
A bullish hammer at major support after a 30% decline is a strong signal.
The same hammer in the middle of an uptrend is just a normal day.

Ask: Is this pattern at a meaningful location — known support, resistance, moving average, Fibonacci level?

### Rule 2: Volume Confirms or Denies
Reversal candle on above-average volume: strong signal
Reversal candle on below-average volume: weak signal, needs confirmation

### Rule 3: Timeframe Hierarchy
A bearish pattern on a weekly chart overrides a bullish pattern on a daily chart.
Always identify the trend on higher timeframes before trading patterns on lower timeframes.

### Rule 4: Trend Context
In a strong uptrend, bearish candles are less reliable — they are more likely to be absorbed.
In a strong downtrend, bullish candles are less reliable — they are more likely to be sold.
Trade reversal patterns most aggressively when they appear at the END of extended moves.

### Rule 5: Confluence Multiplies Reliability
The most reliable signals occur when multiple things align:
- Reversal candle at support
- On above-average volume
- At a Fibonacci level
- After an extended trend
- With a divergence in RSI or MACD
Each additional confluence factor increases the probability of the pattern working.

## Practical Application

**For scalpers:** Use 1-5 minute charts. Focus on engulfing patterns and doji at VWAP. Volume is critical.

**For day traders:** Use 5-15 minute charts. Morning star/evening star at the open. Engulfing patterns at the day's range extremes.

**For swing traders:** Use daily charts. Hammer at 50-day MA. Engulfing at major support. Three white soldiers after a base.

**For position traders:** Use weekly charts. Patterns at long-term support or major Fibonacci levels. Volume must be clearly elevated.
