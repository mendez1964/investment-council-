# On-Chain Analysis — Complete Guide

## What Is On-Chain Analysis
Reading the Bitcoin and Ethereum blockchain to understand who is buying, selling, holding, and moving coins. Unlike price charts which show what happened, on-chain shows WHY it happened and what's likely next.

## MVRV Ratio — Cycle Valuation
**Market Value / Realized Value**
- Realized Value = every coin valued at the price it last moved (network cost basis)
- MVRV tells you: how much profit is the average holder sitting on?

| MVRV | Interpretation |
|------|---------------|
| > 3.5 | Cycle TOP territory — extreme greed, smart money selling |
| 2.5 - 3.5 | Late bull market — be cautious, take profits |
| 1.5 - 2.5 | Mid bull market — healthy, hold |
| 1.0 - 1.5 | Fair value — accumulation zone |
| < 1.0 | CYCLE BOTTOM — average holder at loss, capitulation |
| < 0.8 | Extreme undervaluation — generational buy historically |

## SOPR — Spent Output Profit Ratio
Measures whether coins being spent are at profit or loss:
- **SOPR > 1:** Coins being moved are at profit (holders selling into strength)
- **SOPR = 1:** Breakeven (important support level in bull markets)
- **SOPR < 1:** Coins moved are at loss (forced selling, capitulation)
- **SOPR bouncing off 1.0 in bull market:** Strong support signal
- **SOPR crashing below 1.0 and staying there:** Bear market confirmation

## Exchange Flows — Most Actionable Signal
**Exchange Inflows (deposits TO exchanges):**
- Coins moving to exchanges = preparing to sell
- Large inflow spike = bearish signal (selling pressure incoming)
- Sustained high inflows = distribution phase

**Exchange Outflows (withdrawals FROM exchanges):**
- Coins leaving exchanges = moving to cold storage / self-custody
- Large outflow = bullish signal (accumulation, removing selling pressure)
- Exchange reserves declining = long-term bullish

**Exchange Reserve (total BTC on all exchanges):**
- Declining reserves = bullish (supply being removed from sell pressure)
- Rising reserves = bearish (supply returning to potential sell)
- Current BTC on exchanges ~11-13% of supply (historically low = bullish)

## Long-Term Holder vs Short-Term Holder Supply
**Long-Term Holders (LTH):** Coins unmoved for 155+ days
**Short-Term Holders (STH):** Coins moved within 155 days

LTH behavior:
- LTH accumulating = strong hands in control = bullish
- LTH distributing (selling into strength) = late cycle warning
- LTH supply at all-time high during bear market = smart money loading

STH behavior:
- STH at loss = recent buyers underwater = potential panic selling
- STH at profit + price at resistance = near-term selling pressure

## Realized Price — The Network Cost Basis
**The most important support level:**
- Average price all Bitcoin was last transacted at
- Historically: price below realized price = capitulation/bear market bottom
- Price returning to realized price from below = recovery

**Realized Price Bands:**
- 1x Realized Price: floor / bear market bottom
- 1.5x: fair value support
- 3x: warning zone
- 5x: historical bull market top

## Hash Rate — Network Security & Miner Conviction
- Rising hash rate = miners investing more = long-term bullish
- Hash rate all-time highs during bear market = remarkable conviction
- Hash rate sudden decline = mining profitability collapse or geographic ban
- **Miner Revenue/Hash ratio:** Declining = miner stress, potential selling

## Miner Behavior
Miners sell BTC to pay electricity bills. Watch:
- **Miner outflows to exchanges:** Spike = miner selling (bearish short-term)
- **Miner reserves:** Declining = selling pressure; rising = accumulation
- **Hash ribbon:** When fast MA crosses above slow MA after compression = historical buy signal (miners recovered, capitulation over)

## Active Addresses and Network Growth
- Rising active addresses = more users = bullish fundamental
- Declining active addresses during price rally = weak rally, divergence warning
- New address growth = network expanding
- Flat addresses during bear market = organic bottom formation (holders not leaving)

## Stablecoin Supply Ratio (SSR)
**Bitcoin market cap / Stablecoin supply:**
- Low SSR = large stablecoin supply relative to Bitcoin = lots of dry powder
- High SSR = stablecoins absorbed into Bitcoin = less buying pressure remaining
- Rising stablecoin supply overall = more ammunition for next rally

## NVT Ratio — Network Value to Transactions
Bitcoin's P/E equivalent:
- **NVT = Market Cap / Daily Transaction Volume (USD)**
- High NVT = overvalued relative to usage (sell signal)
- Low NVT = undervalued relative to usage (buy signal)
- NVT Signal uses 90-day MA of transaction volume

## Puell Multiple — Miner Income vs Historical
**Daily issuance revenue / 365-day MA of daily issuance:**
- High Puell (>4): Miners extremely profitable → top signal
- Low Puell (<0.5): Miners under extreme stress → bottom signal
- Historical: Puell < 0.5 has marked every major bottom
