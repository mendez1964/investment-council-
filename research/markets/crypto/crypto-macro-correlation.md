# Crypto Macro Correlation Guide

## The Core Relationship
Crypto is a high-beta risk asset. In the short to medium term, it moves with global liquidity conditions. In the long term, it follows its own adoption curve.

## Global M2 Money Supply — The Master Signal
**Most important macro indicator for crypto:**
- Global M2 = total money supply across US, EU, China, Japan
- Rising M2 = more money chasing assets = crypto benefits most (highest beta)
- Falling M2 = money contracting = crypto hit hardest

**Lead/lag relationship:**
- M2 leads crypto by approximately 6-12 months
- When M2 turns up = expect crypto bull market in 6-12 months
- When M2 turns down = expect crypto bear market in 6-12 months

**How to track:** TradFi M2 data from FRED, add ECB + BOJ + PBOC for global picture

## Federal Reserve Policy Impact
| Fed Action | Short-term crypto | Long-term crypto |
|-----------|-------------------|------------------|
| Rate cut | Strongly bullish | Bullish (risk-on) |
| Rate hike | Bearish | Bearish (tightening) |
| QE (printing) | Very bullish | Core thesis validation |
| QT (balance sheet reduction) | Very bearish | Reduces liquidity |
| Pivot signal | Extremely bullish | Multiple expansion |

## DXY (Dollar Index) Correlation
**Inverse relationship: DXY up = crypto down**
- DXY measures dollar strength vs basket of currencies
- Strong dollar = global dollar shortage = assets fall (priced in dollars)
- Weak dollar = dollar debasement = hard assets benefit
- DXY 10-year breakdown = historically precedes crypto bull markets
- DXY at 10-year highs = crypto headwinds

**Why:** Dollar-denominated assets globally compete. When dollar rises, money flows to dollar-denominated safety. When dollar falls, flows go to risk, commodities, crypto.

## Nasdaq / QQQ Correlation
**30-day rolling correlation: 0.6-0.8 (high)**
- Crypto and tech stocks often move together
- Both are "long duration" assets (future cash flows)
- Rate-sensitive: rising rates hurt both
- Risk-off events (geopolitical, banking crises) hurt both

**Divergence signal:** When crypto and Nasdaq diverge for 30+ days, watch for convergence.

## Gold Relationship
**Different from Nasdaq correlation:**
- Bitcoin vs Gold: narrative battle for store-of-value demand
- When gold rises on inflation/uncertainty = Bitcoin often rises too
- When gold rises on "flight to safety" = Bitcoin may fall (still seen as risk asset)
- Long-term: Bitcoin gaining on gold's market cap

## ISM Manufacturing PMI
- Leading indicator for business cycle
- PMI > 50 = expansion = risk-on environment
- PMI < 50 = contraction = risk-off
- PMI turning up from below 50 = historically bullish for crypto in following quarters
- Watch: ISM PMI monthly release

## Yield Curve and Crypto
- Inverted yield curve = recession signal = near-term bearish for crypto
- Yield curve steepening = recovery signal = medium-term bullish
- 2-year vs 10-year spread: most watched

## Stablecoin Supply as Crypto-Specific Indicator
Unlike macro indicators, stablecoin supply is crypto-native:
- Rising USDC/USDT supply = more fiat entering crypto ecosystem
- Stablecoin market cap is "dry powder" for Bitcoin/altcoin purchases
- Stablecoin dominance rising = risk-off within crypto
- Tether printing new USDT = historically precedes price rises

## Yen Carry Trade (Arthur Hayes framework)
**USDJPY as crypto signal:**
- USDJPY rising (yen weakening) = carry trade expanding = global risk-on = bullish crypto
- USDJPY falling sharply (yen strengthening) = carry trade unwinding = global risk-off = bearish crypto
- Bank of Japan policy changes = potential shock to carry trade
- Monitor: any BOJ interest rate change statements

## The "Everything Correlates in a Crisis" Rule
During acute risk-off events (COVID, banking crises):
- ALL correlations go to 1
- Everything sells simultaneously
- Crypto often leads the selloff (most liquid, 24/7 market)
- Correlation normalizes within 30-90 days
- Historical pattern: sharp crypto drop in crisis → sharp recovery → new highs if macro recovers

## Crypto-Specific Macro Indicators
These are unique to crypto and not in traditional macro:
1. **Bitcoin ETF daily flows** — institutional participation
2. **Stablecoin supply ratio** — buying power vs market cap
3. **Crypto total market cap vs global M2** — relative size
4. **Exchange reserve trend** — selling vs accumulation
5. **Tether market cap** — liquidity entering crypto ecosystem
