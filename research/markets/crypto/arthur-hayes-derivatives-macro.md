## Derivatives Market Structure

### Funding Rates — The Most Important Short-Term Indicator
Perpetual futures (perps) use funding rates to keep price near spot:
- **Positive funding (+):** Longs pay shorts → more longs than shorts → BULLISH but overleveraged
- **Negative funding (-):** Shorts pay longs → more shorts than longs → BEARISH but contrarian BUY signal
- **Extreme positive (>0.1% per 8hr):** Cascade liquidation risk for longs
- **Extreme negative (<-0.05% per 8hr):** Short squeeze setup

**Annualized equivalent:** 0.03%/8hr = ~33% annualized cost to hold long

### Open Interest — Total Leverage in Market
- Rising OI + rising price = healthy bull trend
- Rising OI + falling price = bearish (shorts adding)
- Falling OI + rising price = rally on short covering (less sustainable)
- Falling OI + falling price = capitulation/deleveraging (often marks lows)
- **OI spikes** precede major liquidation events

### Liquidation Levels
Exchanges publish liquidation heatmaps:
- Large clusters of liquidations above price = "liquidation magnets" upside
- Large clusters below price = downside risk
- Price moves toward liquidity (the market hunts stops)

### Basis — Cash vs Futures Premium/Discount
- Contango (futures > spot): bullish market paying premium for future exposure
- Backwardation (futures < spot): bearish, institutions unwilling to pay premium
- Extreme contango >20% annualized: historically precedes corrections
- Basis compression: institutional signal that demand is cooling

## The Yen Carry Trade — Arthur's Macro Signal
Most important macro trade affecting crypto:
- Japanese investors borrow yen at 0% interest (BOJ policy)
- Invest in higher-yielding US assets (treasuries, risk assets)
- **When yen strengthens (BOJ raises rates):** Carry trade unwinds → sell risk assets globally → CRYPTO FALLS
- **When yen weakens:** Carry trade expands → more capital in risk assets → CRYPTO RISES
- Watch: USDJPY — rising = yen weakening = good for crypto; falling = yen strengthening = bad

## Dollar Liquidity Framework
Net liquidity formula Arthur uses:
**Fed Balance Sheet - TGA (Treasury General Account) - RRP (Reverse Repo)**
- Rising net liquidity = more dollars in system = risk assets rise
- Falling net liquidity = dollars being absorbed = risk assets fall
- TGA drawdown (Treasury spending) = hidden QE
- RRP draining = money moving from Fed to market = liquidity injection

## Crypto Credit Cycle
Similar to traditional credit cycles:
- **Expansion:** Leverage builds, funding rates positive, OI rising
- **Euphoria:** Max leverage, max funding rates, stablecoins printing
- **Contraction:** Funding negative, forced liquidations
- **Capitulation:** OI collapses, negative funding, forced selling done
- **Recovery:** Low OI, neutral funding, organic buying returns
