# Hedge Funds — Complete Guide

## What Hedge Funds Are
Private investment partnerships for accredited investors and institutions. Minimum investment typically $1 million or more. Can use any strategy, any instrument, long or short, leveraged or not. The standard fee structure: 2% annual management fee plus 20% of profits (the "2 and 20" model). The best funds have generated multi-decade track records that no passive strategy has matched.

## The Major Strategies

### Long/Short Equity
The most common hedge fund strategy. Buy stocks expected to rise (long), short stocks expected to fall (short). Net exposure can be net long, net short, or market neutral.

**Famous practitioners:** Julian Robertson (Tiger Management), Steve Cohen (SAC Capital, now Point72), David Einhorn (Greenlight Capital), Seth Klarman (Baupost)

**What it means for traders:** When a major long/short fund builds a large position, the buying is sustained and methodical — stock price rises on no obvious news, volume is steady but persistent. This is institutional accumulation. The opposite is true for shorts — a stock can decline mysteriously ahead of bad news a fund has identified through deep research.

**Tiger Cubs:** Dozens of successful funds were started by Julian Robertson's former analysts — they are called "Tiger Cubs." When multiple Tiger Cubs buy the same stock, it moves the market. Tracking which funds are Tiger offspring gives insight into investment style.

### Global Macro
Trade based on macroeconomic themes across countries, currencies, interest rates, and commodities. Take massive positions in futures and options. React to interest rate differentials, political events, debt cycles, and current account imbalances.

**Famous practitioners:** George Soros (Quantum Fund), Ray Dalio (Bridgewater), Paul Tudor Jones (Tudor Investment), Stanley Druckenmiller, Louis Bacon (Moore Capital)

**What it means for traders:** Global macro funds move currency and commodity markets in ways that create trends lasting months. They have enormous capital and research capability. When Dalio's models say inflation is coming, he positions for it in size — creating self-reinforcing trends in inflation assets.

**The Soros lesson:** The 1992 British pound trade showed that a single macro fund can overwhelm a central bank's ability to defend an unsustainable position. When macro fundamentals are clear and a major fund is positioned for them, the trend is powerful.

### Quantitative and Algorithmic
Use mathematical models and computer algorithms to find and exploit market inefficiencies. Can be high-frequency (milliseconds), statistical arbitrage (days to weeks), or systematic trend following (weeks to months).

**Famous practitioners:** Renaissance Technologies (Jim Simons) — Medallion Fund averaged 66% annually before fees over 30 years, the greatest track record in investment history. Two Sigma, DE Shaw, Citadel Securities.

**What it means for traders:** Quant funds create patterns in the market through their systematic buying and selling. They amplify momentum (when trend-following algos agree on direction) and create mean reversion (when stat arb models detect divergence). High-frequency trading creates the bid/ask spread cost that retail traders pay on every trade. Understanding that algos are hunting stops at obvious price levels helps explain many seemingly irrational intraday moves.

### Event-Driven
Trade around specific corporate events: mergers, acquisitions, bankruptcies, spinoffs, special dividends, earnings surprises, regulatory decisions.

**Famous practitioners:** Bill Ackman (Pershing Square), Carl Icahn (Icahn Capital), Dan Loeb (Third Point), Paul Singer (Elliott Management)

**Activist investing:** These funds buy large stakes in companies and then publicly demand changes — new management, sale of the company, spinoffs, buybacks. When an activist announces a stake, stocks typically pop 5-20% immediately. Trading ahead of known activist targets (using 13F data and research) is a legitimate edge.

**What it means for traders:** Merger arbitrage creates specific price behavior — target stocks trade slightly below the announced deal price, reflecting deal completion risk. Acquiring company often falls initially. Knowing the typical merger arb spread and timeline provides trading opportunities.

### Multi-Strategy
Run multiple strategies simultaneously in different internal "pods" or teams. Each pod operates independently with allocated capital. Famous: Millennium Management (Izzy Englander), Citadel (Ken Griffin), Balyasny Asset Management, Point72.

**What it means for traders:** Multi-strategy funds are the most sophisticated participants. They have teams covering every strategy, every sector, every geography simultaneously. They adapt faster than single-strategy funds. Their prime brokerage data gives prime brokers insight into what is happening across the entire market.

## Tracking Hedge Fund Positions: 13F Filings

Every investment manager with over $100 million in US equity assets must file Form 13F with the SEC within 45 days of each quarter end. This reveals their long US equity positions.

**What 13Fs show:** Long stock positions, call options (bullish), put options (may be hedges).
**What 13Fs do NOT show:** Short positions, bonds, futures, international stocks, any position exited before quarter end.

**The 45-day delay:** You are seeing positions as they existed up to 45 days ago. The fund may have already exited. Still valuable for understanding investment thesis and style.

**Best free 13F tools:**
- WhaleWisdom.com: Tracks changes quarter over quarter, shows conviction (% of portfolio), new buys and complete sells
- Dataroma.com: "Superinvestor" portfolios — Buffett, Ackman, Klarman, Loeb and others
- SEC EDGAR directly: sec.gov → EDGAR → search by fund name

**What to look for in 13F data:**
- New position initiated: Fund saw opportunity where none existed before
- Position significantly increased: Conviction growing
- Position significantly decreased: Conviction waning (warning for longs)
- Position eliminated: Fund completely exited — potential issue
- Multiple top funds buying same stock: Highest conviction signal — independent analysis reached same conclusion

## Famous Hedge Fund Trades to Study

**George Soros / British Pound 1992:** Understanding reflexivity applied to currency pegs. Made $1B in one day.

**John Paulson / Subprime 2007:** Bought CDS protection on mortgage securities before the crisis. Made $15B. Best hedge fund trade in history.

**Michael Burry / Subprime 2005-2007:** Read the actual loan prospectuses. Made $700M for Scion Capital.

**David Einhorn / Lehman Brothers 2008:** Publicly questioned Lehman's accounting in May 2008 and shorted the stock. Was right — Lehman failed in September.

**Bill Ackman / Herbalife 2012:** Publicly announced $1B short position calling it a pyramid scheme. Became a multi-year battle with Carl Icahn on the long side. Eventually covered at a loss — a reminder that even the best can be wrong.

**Ackman / COVID 2020:** Purchased $27M in credit default swaps as pandemic insurance. Made $2.6 billion when credit markets collapsed in March 2020. Then reinvested proceeds in equities near the bottom. The single greatest trade of 2020.
